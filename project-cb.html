01. Createbase Case Study

Timeline

2.5 weeks

Platform

Responsive website

My Role

UI/UX Designer

Front End Developer

Introduction

CreateBase is a startup that provides an online educational platform enabling students to create authentic solutions to real-world problems using 3D simulations and digital robotics. The platform provides teaching material for students to learn STEAM subjects and the basics of coding. This new version of the website set out to improve the experience of the website, demonstrate the value of the product better to target users, establish the company’s branding and increase sign-ups to the platform.

My Role

I led the wireframing, low and high fidelity mockups, user testing, and front end development for this project.

I collaborated with the marketing coordinator (Weiwei) and product owner (Brydon) for early ideation and content management and the backend engineer (Carl) for implementation and testing.

Problem

The previous version of the website suffered from many problems as the business pivoted in its search for product-market fit.

Defining the problem

Goals

The problem was broken down into three smaller goals to improve teachers’ confidence to sign-up and try our platform.

Improve product understanding

Design the new website using specific user journeys that cater to a range of teachers. Due to technology teachers' different priorities, we needed to consider that they all have different teaching styles, curriculums, school-specific content structures, and other factors. We wanted our product to resonate with the needs of teachers. The flow of information and user journeys were essential.

Relevant "at a glance" content

Conveying the core product offerings clearly whilst showcasing important features efficiently to teachers is crucial to giving them more confidence in registering and trying out the platform. Teachers had told us about the importance of their spare time, especially when looking for new resources.

Consistency is key

Utilise the power of familiarity by making the website and platform components and web stack consistent. This reduced the learning curve of the new platform and make it easier for users to start using the product right away. Alongside this ease of use, usage speed and retention from having a smooth experience can improve for different users.

Our customers

These customer personas were created using the insights and observations from interviewing teachers and live and online school demos.

User flows and Wireframes

Provided are some of many versions of the early website flows, low and high fidelity prototypes created in Figma. The initial website flows were created using different approaches based on the different personas created. At this stage my main goal is to diverge first, then converge. As the flow of the content was one of the priorities, this was combined and iterated multiple times before the low fidelity mockups began.

Usability Testing and Customer Interviews

To test our prototypes and validate our design decisions, we experimented with teachers remotely during lockdowns, ensuring the safety of all parties.

Several semi-unstructured observational tests were conducted, primarily observing how users browse the home page Figma prototype whilst they speak aloud what they are thinking throughout the process. Essential insights and behaviours were documented and shared with the team. This test was mainly for qualitative feedback and provided valuable insights into the home page’s usability.

Goals of testing

  • Understand if the new home page is exciting enough to users to encourage them to sign-up for the platform

  • Understand if the content of the home page is structured well enough for users to have a better understanding of the product

Actionable Outcomes

  • Edit the home page mockup to improve the user experience and content

  • Edit the videos to provide the best experience

  • Be able to confidently implement a home page with justifiable reasoning for content decisions

Implementation

I was solely responsible for the coding implementation of the website after the design process. The website was coded using ReactJS, TypeScript, NextJS, SASS, Express, HTML, CSS, Git.

Outcomes

After implementation of the website, the total registered users had a weekly growth rate of 120% to the platform. This led to 200+ active users spread throughout schools in NZ, AUS and the UK in the first 2 months of release.

Social media posts celebrating sign-up success

What we learned

Proving the efficacy of user testing in a fast moving environment

When working in a fast-paced startup environment, the constant want to develop and find results can lead to essential processes like user testing being underestimated in its effectiveness for the business. I met with the team to discuss the value of saving potential development time and reducing technical debt made possible by testing and how it should have been a higher priority.

After this process, there was praise for how effective the testing results were. Team members stated that seeing the interactions with our products from teachers and the results provided great insights that confirmed or challenged their previous assumptions about our users. Insights like these helped ensure that we were providing teachers with the best experience for their different needs.

Taking a step back

I made sure that we kept in mind the goals at every point of the design process and avoided getting too lost on the macro and forgetting to look at the big picture. Meeting with members from different teams helped re-align and provide helpful feedback on improvements to the website and the surrounding campaign.

Design System

The CreateBase logo was designed to have an optical illusion quality. Part 2D and part 3D represent the possibilities we create and try to achieve.

An interplay between a spanner and nut speaks to the nature of the brand and our product lines of creating, building and play. The round-edged icon for a gentle, inviting appearance.

The colours blue denote trust, and purple represents diversity with subliminal reinforcement of the brand intent -possibilities and ambiguity.
