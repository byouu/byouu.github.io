Introduction

CreateBase is a startup that provides an online educational platform aimed at enabling students to create authentic solutions to real-world problems using 3D simulations and digital robotics. The platform provides teaching material for students to learn STEAM subjects and the basics of coding. This new website was set out to improve the experience of the website, demonstrate the value of the product better to target users, better establish the company’s branding and increase sign ups to the platform.

My Role

For this project I led the wireframing, low and high fidelity mockups, user testing and front end development.

I collaborated with the marketing coordinator (Weiwei) and product owner (Brydon) for early ideation and content management and backend engineer (Carl) for implementation and testing.

Problem

The old version of the website suffered from many problems as the business pivoted in it's search for product market fit.

Defining the problem

Goals

Improve product understanding

Design the new website using specific user journeys that cater to a range of customers. Due to the different priorities that technology teachers have considering their teaching styles, curriculum, school-specific content structure, and other factors. The flow of information and user journeys are important.

Relevant "at a glance" content

Conveying the core product offerings clearly whilst showcasing important features to teachers efficiently is crucial to giving them more confidence in registering and trying out the platform.

Consistency is key

Utilise the power of familiarity by making the website and platform components and web stack consistent. This will reduce the learning curve of the new platform and make it easier for users to start using the product right away. Alongside this ease of use, usage speed and retention from having a smooth experience can improve for different users.

Our customers

These customer personas were created using the insights and observations we had gathered from interviewing teachers and live and online schools demos.

Process

Provided are some of many versions of the early website flows, low and high fidelity prototypes created in Figma. The initial website flows were created using different approaches based on the different personas that were created. As the flow of the content was one of the priorities this was combined and iterated on multiple times before the low fidelity mockups began.

Usability Testing and Customer Interviews

To test our prototypes and validate our design decisions, we tested with teachers remotely keeping all parties safe.

I completed several semi unstructured observational tests primarily observing how a user browses the home page figma prototype whilst they speak out loud what they are thinking throughout the process. Important insights and behaviours were noted. This test was mainly for qualitative feedback and provided useful insights on the usability of the home page.

Goals of testing

  • Understand if the new home page is exciting enough to users to encourage them sign up to the platform

  • Understand if the content of the home page is structured well enough for users to have a good understanding of the product

Actionable Outcomes

  • Edit the home page mock up to improve the user experience and content

  • Edit the videos to provide the best experience

  • Be able to confidently implement a home page with justifiable reasoning for content decisions

Implementation

I was solely responsible for the coding implementation of the website after the design process. The website was coded using ReactJS, TypeScript, NextJS, SASS, Express, HTML, CSS, Git

Outcomes

Currently the new website has just been deployed with tracking data waiting to be analysed. Initial feedback from customers of the new website have been largely positive.

What we learned

A reiteration on the importance of user testing

The insights from these user tests were beneficial when conducting these user tests, with some previous assumptions challenged and changed. This helped to ensure that we were providing teachers with the best experience for their different needs.

Taking a step back

Taking a look at the larger picture with other members from different teams helped to re-align and provide useful feedback on improvements to the website.

Color System

The CreateBase logo was designed to have an optical illusion quality. Part 2D part 3D represents the possibilities we are creating and trying to achieve.

An interplay between a spanner and nut speaks to the nature of the brand and our product lines. Round edges to add a gentle look as a subliminal nod to diversity in STEM fields.

Colours blue denote trust, and purple represents diversity with subliminal reinforcement of the brand intent -possibilities and ambiguity.
